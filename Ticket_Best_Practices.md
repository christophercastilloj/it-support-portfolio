Technical Support Handbook: PSA & Ticket Management Best Practices

Introduction

Effective ticket management ensures that we meet our SLA (Service Level Agreement) targets and provide high-quality support to our global team.

Ticket Lifecycle Standards

1. Initial Response (The 1-Hour Rule)

All support emails/tickets must be acknowledged within 1 hour during business hours.

Status: Change from To Do to In Progress.

2. Categorization & Labels

To identify trends and bugs, every ticket must include appropriate labels:

#bug - Platform software issues.

#access - Login or permission requests.

#hardware - Physical equipment failures.

#training - User needs guidance on how to use a feature.

3. Communication Tone

Always provide clear, accurate, and actionable guidance.

Avoid: "It's fixed."

Prefer: "The root cause was a DNS mismatch. I have flushed the cache, and you should be able to access the site now. Please let me know if it works on your end."

4. Resolution & Documentation

Resolution Field: Describe exactly how the issue was fixed.

Knowledge Base: If the issue has occurred more than 3 times, create a "How-to" guide in the internal FAQ.

Tracking & Trends

Weekly, the Technical Support team will review the "Ticket Log" to surface feature requests to the engineering team based on recurring user pain points.
